HISTORY OF VAISHALI DISTRICT

Vaishali district, located in the state of Bihar, India, has a rich historical and cultural significance. It is known as one of the most ancient places in India and holds a prominent place in the history of Buddhism and Jainism. 1. Ancient History: Origin of the Name: The name Vaishali is derived from King Vishal of the Mahabharata period, who is believed to have ruled here. It is said that the city was named Vaishali after him. Birthplace of Mahavira: Vaishali is famously known as the birthplace of Lord Mahavira, the 24th Tirthankara of Jainism, who was born in Kundalagram (a village in Vaishali) in 599 BCE. Buddhism Significance: Gautama Buddha also visited Vaishali multiple times. He preached his last sermon at Kolhua, near Vaishali, and announced his approaching Mahaparinirvana. Republican State: Vaishali is considered one of the world's first democratic republics (Vajji Sangha) around 600 BCE, where the people collectively elected their ruler. Amrapali's Story: Amrapali, a beautiful courtesan, belonged to Vaishali. Later, she became a devoted follower of Lord Buddha.

2. Medieval History: During the Gupta dynasty, Vaishali continued to flourish as a center of culture and trade. It later came under the rule of the Maurya dynasty during the reign of Chandragupta Maurya and Ashoka the Great.
3. Modern History: In British India, Vaishali was part of the Tirhut division. After India's independence in 1947, Vaishali became a part of Bihar and was officially declared a district in 1972.
